# Whisper Coaching Knowledge Base
## Common Misdiagnoses Reference
*Internal document — not exposed in UI. Used to inform Whisper's system prompt and coaching logic.*

---

### Purpose

Golfers frequently misidentify the root cause of their miss. If Whisper simply validates the golfer's self-diagnosis, it risks reinforcing incorrect compensations and grooving a worse swing. This reference table maps the most common perceived causes to the most likely actual causes, and provides Whisper with the correct coaching direction.

**Core rule:** Whisper never echoes the golfer's diagnosis back as confirmation. It listens, observes the pattern, and steers toward the inferred cause — but only once enough data exists to justify it.

---

## Table 1: Ball Flight Misdiagnoses

| Golfer Says | What They Think Is Wrong | What Is Actually More Likely Wrong | Whisper's Direction |
|---|---|---|---|
| "I'm slicing it" | Open clubface at address | Outside-in swing path (over the top) | Focus on swing path first, not grip. Encourage feeling the club drop inside on the downswing. |
| "I'm slicing it" | Weak grip | Early extension — hips moving toward ball through impact | Check hip movement, not just hand position. |
| "I'm hooking it" | Closed clubface | Too much hand rotation through impact (flipping) | Encourage quieter hands, lead wrist staying flat. |
| "I'm hooking it" | Strong grip | Swing path too far inside-out, clubface not catching up | Check swing direction first before adjusting grip. |
| "I'm pushing it right" | Aimed too far right | Body stopping through impact, arms taking over | Encourage full rotation, body clearing through the shot. |
| "I'm pulling it left" | Aimed too far left | Over-the-top swing path — same root cause as a slice | Same fix as the slice — path correction, not aim adjustment. |
| "I keep missing right with my irons but straight with my driver" | Different clubs, different problems | Likely ball position issue — irons too far forward in stance | Suggest moving ball position back slightly for irons. |

## Table 2: Contact Misdiagnoses

| Golfer Says | What They Think Is Wrong | What Is Actually More Likely Wrong | Whisper's Direction |
|---|---|---|---|
| "I'm chunking it — I must be dipping" | Head moving down during swing | Early release (casting) — club bottoms out before ball | Fix the release, not the head. Encourage maintaining lag longer. |
| "I'm chunking it — my weight isn't shifting" | Weight staying on back foot | Early extension — hips moving toward ball, causing steep angle | Check hip movement through impact zone. |
| "I'm thinning it — I must be lifting my head" | Head coming up during swing | Early extension or loss of posture in downswing | Keep the posture, don't force the head down — it's a symptom not a cause. |
| "I'm shanking it — my swing is too steep" | Steep swing plane | Standing too close to ball, or swaying toward ball on downswing | Check setup distance and lateral sway — not swing plane. |
| "I'm shanking it — I'm coming over the top" | Outside-in path | Club moving away from body through impact (early extension) | Opposite fix to over the top — encourage club staying close to body. |
| "I'm hitting it off the toe — I must be standing too far away" | Setup distance | Arms extending too early through impact, straightening out | Maintain arm bend slightly longer through impact. |
| "I'm hitting off the heel — I must be standing too close" | Setup distance | Swaying toward the ball on the downswing | Check lateral movement, not starting position. |
| "I'm topping it — I'm not getting underneath the ball" | Need to hit up on the ball | Loss of posture — standing up through impact | Stay in the posture set at address. Never try to lift the ball. |

## Table 3: Distance & Trajectory Misdiagnoses

| Golfer Says | What They Think Is Wrong | What Is Actually More Likely Wrong | Whisper's Direction |
|---|---|---|---|
| "I'm not getting enough distance — I need to swing harder" | Not enough power | Poor contact — off-centre strikes lose far more distance than swing speed | Focus on strike quality, not effort. Centre contact beats swing speed every time. |
| "My ball flight is too high — I'm hitting up on it too much" | Angle of attack | Too much dynamic loft — hands not ahead of ball at impact | Encourage forward shaft lean at impact, hands leading. |
| "My ball flight is too low — I'm hitting down too much" | Steep angle | Early release — loft is being added through impact | Maintain lag, stop casting. |
| "I'm losing distance as I fatigue — my technique breaks down" | Tiredness affecting mechanics | Often correct, but the specific failure is usually trail elbow breaking down first | Monitor trail elbow position as session progresses. |
| "I hit my 7 iron further than my 6 — must be a loft issue" | Club loft difference | Likely better strike quality with the 7 — confidence club | Encourage same setup and tempo with 6. The club is fine. |

## Table 4: Mental & Tempo Misdiagnoses

| Golfer Says | What They Think Is Wrong | What Is Actually More Likely Wrong | Whisper's Direction |
|---|---|---|---|
| "I play better on the course than at the range — range must not suit me" | Range environment | Often: range balls fly differently, OR the golfer over-thinks at the range | Validate the observation, encourage treating each range shot like a real shot with a target. |
| "I play worse under pressure — I must get nervous" | Nerves / mental weakness | Grip pressure increasing under pressure, causing tension throughout | Suggest monitoring grip pressure as the primary physical indicator of tension. |
| "My swing breaks down when I swing easy — I need more tempo" | Tempo | Loss of structure when effort reduces — often wrist conditions change | Maintain wrist conditions at all effort levels. |
| "I'm rushing — I need to slow my backswing down" | Backswing speed | Transition is the actual rush — the downswing starts before backswing completes | Focus on completing the backswing, not slowing it. Feel the pause at the top. |
| "I hit better with an older swing thought — must go back to basics" | Current technique is wrong | Older swing thought may have addressed the same root cause more intuitively | Ask what the old cue was — it may still be the correct fix. |
| "I'm inconsistent — my swing changes every day" | Technique instability | Often: setup variables changing (grip, stance width, ball position) not the swing itself | Check setup first before diagnosing swing change. |

## Table 5: Club-Specific Misdiagnoses

| Club | Golfer Says | What Is Actually More Likely Wrong | Whisper's Direction |
|---|---|---|---|
| Driver | "I slice my driver but not my irons" | Ball too far forward, face open at impact for driver specifically | Check tee height and ball position before swing changes. |
| Driver | "I need to tee it lower to stop ballooning" | Dynamic loft too high — hands not leading | Tee height rarely fixes ballooning — fix the impact position. |
| Long irons | "Long irons are just hard — I should hit hybrids" | Technique issue that a hybrid will mask not fix | Work the long iron pattern — downwind should help fix it, not avoid it. |
| Wedges | "I need to open the face more to get backspin" | Strike quality — backspin comes from clean contact, not open face | Hit down and through on a clean strike first. Face angle is secondary. |
| Wedges | "I chunk my chips — short game is my weakness" | Usually setup: too much weight on back foot, ball too far back | Check setup before technique. Weight forward, ball centre to slightly back. |
| Putter | "I miss left — must be a pull" | Face angle at impact is open or path is outside-in | Most putting misses are face angle not path — check face at impact. |

## Whisper's Misdiagnosis Protocol

When a golfer expresses a perceived cause, Whisper follows this internal process:

1. **Log the perceived cause** in the golfer's profile as a self-reported belief — not a fact.
2. **Cross-reference** with this table to identify whether the perceived cause aligns with the likely actual cause.
3. **Do not immediately contradict** the golfer. Whisper observes first.
4. **Wait for pattern repetition** — a single shot does not validate any diagnosis, inferred or perceived.
5. **When pattern is established**, Whisper gently introduces the inferred cause: *"You've mentioned grip a few times, but I've noticed the miss tends to happen when you rush the transition — want to try focusing on tempo instead?"*
6. **Never validate a wrong diagnosis** even if the golfer is confident. Silence or deflection is preferable to false confirmation.
7. **Track which cues work** — if a cue based on the inferred cause produces improvement, weight the inferred cause more heavily in future sessions.

## Priority Misdiagnoses to Detect Early

These are the most common and most damaging incorrect compensations. Whisper should be especially alert to these:

| Misdiagnosis | Why It's Dangerous | Early Signal |
|---|---|---|
| "I'm slicing — need a stronger grip" | Gripping stronger without fixing path makes the hook/snap hook a real risk | Golfer mentions grip change after a slice |
| "I'm chunking — need to keep my head down" | Forcing the head down increases early extension | Golfer focuses on head position after fat shots |
| "I'm topping — need to get under the ball" | Trying to lift creates the exact topped shot they're avoiding | Golfer mentions trying to "scoop" or "help" the ball up |
| "I'm shanking — I'm too steep" | Steep is rarely the shank cause — wrong compensation grooves the miss | Golfer mentions standing further away after a shank |
| "I need to swing harder for more distance" | More effort = more tension = worse contact | Golfer mentions effort or power after distance complaint |
